🔬 For specialists: advanced quantum tools

⚙️ QUANTUM OPEN LAB

Visual quantum circuit editor with real Python execution (Qiskit). Design and simulate quantum circuits, save them to your account.

📓 QUANTUM NOTEBOOK

Create and edit complete quantum notebooks. Export to .ipynb. Only founders and administrators review before publishing to Projects. It combines with Quantum Open Lab in circuit cells to create quantum algorithms without needing to program.

PROJECTS

Explore and publish quantum computing projects. Share ideas, algorithms, circuits and interactive notebook booklets with the QCOL community.

🛠️ QUANTUM APP STUDIO

Development environment to build Quantum Apps. Uses HTML for the interface and Python logic with quantum libraries Cirq, Qiskit, PennyLane already available. Create, test and deploy new quantum tools. Access it through Quantum Puzzle.

📘❓ What is an interactive notebook booklet?
It is a new educational standard that combines the theory of a book, the practice of programming, and the experimentation of a laboratory. You can run the notebook without modifying the theory, write in its markdown cells, execute code, make changes without saving them, and reset to the original code at any time.

💰 Business model: sustainable & fair

🛒

Quantum Puzzle Store

Developers publish Quantum Apps or tools. QCOL takes a 20% commission on each sale or subscription. The rest goes to the developer. We grow the worldwide ecosystem of quantum solutions, expanding access and usage for everyone.

🔧

Customization layers for manufacturers

Hardware vendors can integrate their quantum processors for free (we don't resell APIs). They pay for custom adaptation layers and dedicated support.

🏢

Private apps for companies & institutions

Organizations can deploy private Quantum Apps/tools inside the ecosystem, accessible only by their members. They pay a hosting and maintenance fee.

🛡️

App Quality & Ecosystem Health

Useless or malicious apps naturally fail: users won't buy inefficient apps that waste quantum hardware or deliver no value. In addition, QCOL performs reviews and community beta testing. Good solutions are promoted organically through incentives, ratings, and transparent supervision. This ensures only useful, well‑built apps thrive.

✅ Integration of quantum hardware is always free. We believe in open access, not reselling APIs. Our revenue comes from value-added services, marketplace commissions, and private deployments.

🆚 How QCOL is different from other quantum operating systems

Most existing quantum operating systems (like Origin Pilot, Wuyue, or European/US research OS) are designed to manage local quantum hardware and require deep programming skills. They are not cloud-native, not app‑centric, and not built for mass adoption.

☁️

Cloud‑native, not hardware‑local

QCOL runs entirely in the cloud. You don't need your own quantum computer. Other OSs are tied to specific local machines (e.g., Origin Pilot for spin‑based quantum chips).

📱

App ecosystem, not just an API

QCOL is a complete app store and runtime for end‑users. Others are low‑level APIs for programmers – you still need to write quantum code.

🌍

Hardware‑agnostic by design

We integrate all vendors (IBM, Google, Amazon, Chinese manufacturers) into one universal layer. Competitors are usually locked to their own or a single nation's hardware.

🧑‍🎓

Focused on end‑users, not just specialists

Our goal is zero‑expertise adoption. Other quantum OSs are built for researchers who already know linear algebra and programming. They ignore the other 99% of potential users.

🇨🇳 What about China's strategy (DeepSeek, Origin Pilot, Wuyue)?
China is aggressively developing its own quantum operating systems (Origin Pilot, Wuyue) and AI models (DeepSeek) to break Western dominance and establish a Chinese-led global standard. The strategy is twofold: first, achieve technological self-sufficiency to bypass US/EU sanctions; second, create a parallel quantum ecosystem that could undercut American and European quantum cloud services with cheaper, state-backed alternatives. However, like their Western counterparts, these Chinese OSs remain local, hardware‑specific, and require expert programming. They are not designed for mass adoption nor for end‑users. DeepSeek's open‑source AI approach hints at a similar quantum play: release basic tools for free to capture developers, then lock enterprises into a proprietary Chinese stack. None of them is building a neutral, cloud‑native, app‑centric ecosystem that works across all vendors. That is exactly what QCOL does – a truly open, hardware‑agnostic platform for the entire world.
